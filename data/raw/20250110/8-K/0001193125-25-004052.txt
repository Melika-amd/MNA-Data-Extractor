 <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold">ITEM&#8201;8.01 Other Events. </p> <p style="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On January&#160;8, 2025, Intra-Cellular Therapies, Inc. (the &#8220;Company&#8221;) entered into an agreement with Sandoz Inc. (&#8220;Sandoz&#8221;) resolving patent litigation related to the Company&#8217;s product CAPLYTA<sup style="font-size:75%; vertical-align:top">&#174;</sup> (lumateperone), which is approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (the &#8220;FDA&#8221;) for the adult treatment of schizophrenia and for the adult treatment of depressive episodes associated with bipolar I or II disorder (bipolar depression) as monotherapy and as adjunctive therapy with lithium or valproate. The pending patent litigation was filed by the Company in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey in response to Sandoz notifying the Company that it had submitted an Abbreviated New Drug Application (&#8220;ANDA&#8221;) to the FDA seeking approval to market generic versions of CAPLYTA prior to expiration of the Company&#8217;s Orange Book listed patents. As a result of this settlement agreement, the Company has granted Sandoz the right to sell generic versions of CAPLYTA in the United States beginning July&#160;1, 2040, or earlier under certain circumstances. As required by law, the Company will submit the agreement to the U.S. Federal Trade Commission and the U.S. Department of Justice. Similar patent litigation brought by the Company against other parties remains pending in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey. </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Company&#8217;s press release announcing the settlement agreement with Sandoz is filed as Exhibit&#160;99.1 to this Current Report on <span style="white-space:nowrap">Form&#160;8-K</span> and is incorporated herein by reference. </p> <p style="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold">ITEM&#8201;9.01 Financial Statements and Exhibits. </p> <p style="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">(d)<span style="text-decoration:underline">&#160;Exhibits</span> </p> <p style="font-size:12pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&#160;</p>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Intra-Cellular Therapies Settles CAPLYTA<SUP STYLE="font-size:75%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> (lumateperone) Patent Litigation
with&nbsp;Sandoz</B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>BEDMINSTER,</B><B></B>&nbsp;N.J., Jan. 10, 2025 (GLOBE NEWSWIRE) &#151;&nbsp;Intra-Cellular Therapies, Inc.&nbsp;(Nasdaq: ITCI), a
biopharmaceutical company focused on&nbsp;the development and commercialization of therapeutics for central nervous system (CNS) disorders, today announced that it has entered into&nbsp;a settlement agreement&nbsp;with&nbsp;Sandoz&nbsp;Inc. (Sandoz)
resolving patent litigation related to Intra-Cellular Therapies&#146; product&nbsp;CAPLYTA<SUP STYLE="font-size:75%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> (lumateperone). The&nbsp;litigation, which is pending in the U.S. District Court for the District of
New Jersey, resulted from submission by&nbsp;Sandoz&nbsp;of an Abbreviated New Drug Application&nbsp;to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration seeking approval to market a generic equivalent of CAPLYTA in the United States. The settlement
agreement&nbsp;permits&nbsp;Sandoz&nbsp;to begin selling generic versions of CAPLYTA on July&nbsp;1, 2040, or earlier under certain circumstances. As required by law, Intra-Cellular Therapies will submit the agreement to the U.S. Federal Trade
Commission and the U.S. Department of Justice. Similar patent litigation brought by Intra-Cellular Therapies against other parties remains pending in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey.</P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>About&nbsp;Intra-Cellular Therapies </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Intra-Cellular
Therapies&nbsp;is a biopharmaceutical company <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">founded&nbsp;on&nbsp;Nobel&nbsp;prize-winning&nbsp;research</FONT> that allows us to understand how therapies affect the inner-workings of cells in the body. The company
leverages this intracellular approach to develop innovative treatments for people living with complex psychiatric and neurologic diseases. For more information, please visit<U></U><U>&nbsp;www.intracellulartherapies.com</U>. </P>
