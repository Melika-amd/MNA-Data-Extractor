<td style="width:11%;vertical-align:top" align="left"><span style="font-weight:bold">Item&#8201;2.05.</span></td>
<td align="left" style="vertical-align:top"> <p style=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold;text-align:left"><span style="text-decoration:underline">Costs Associated with Exit or Disposal Activities</span>. </p></td></tr></table> <p style="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On January&#160;9, 2025, Kohl&#8217;s Corporation (&#8220;Kohl&#8217;s&#8221; or the &#8220;Company&#8221;) announced certain real estate and workforce rationalization actions to optimize its retail footprint and realign the Company&#8217;s workforce. Kohl&#8217;s will close its San Bernardino <span style="white-space:nowrap">E-commerce</span> Fulfillment Center (EFC) when the lease on that facility expires in May 2025, as well as 27 underperforming stores by April 2025. These measures are part of the Company&#8217;s ongoing effort to increase efficiency and support the health and future of its business. </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The San Bernardino EFC is one of 15 EFCs and Distribution Centers in Kohl&#8217;s nationwide supply chain network. In recent years, the Company has increased efficiencies with advanced technology capabilities at newer EFC facilities and expanded its ability to fulfill customer orders from store locations, allowing the Company to continue fulfilling customer orders without the San Bernardino EFC. While Kohl&#8217;s continues to believe in the health and strength of its profitable store base, the stores identified for closure are underperforming and represent approximately 2.3% of the Company&#8217;s more than 1,150 locations. Impacted associates have been informed and offered a competitive severance package or the opportunity to apply to other open roles at Kohl&#8217;s. </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Kohl&#8217;s currently estimates that it will recognize cumulative <span style="white-space:nowrap">pre-tax</span> charges of approximately $60&#160;million to $80&#160;million, including $30&#160;million of <span style="white-space:nowrap">non-cash</span> charges in real estate and other asset-related charges, costs, and impairments and $30&#160;million to $50&#160;million of cash expenditures related to associate severance, benefits, and other exit costs.
