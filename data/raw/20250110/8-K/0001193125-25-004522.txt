 <p style="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:bold">Item&#8201;3.01. Notice of Delisting or Failure to Satisfy a Continued Listing Rule or Standard; Transfer of Listing. </p> <p style="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">On January&#160;9, 2025, DURECT Corporation, a Delaware Corporation (the &#8220;Company&#8221;), received a letter (the &#8220;Notice&#8221;) from the Listing Qualifications Department of the Nasdaq Stock Market (&#8220;Nasdaq&#8221;) informing the Company that, because the closing bid price for the Company&#8217;s common stock listed on Nasdaq was below $1.00 for 30 consecutive trading days, the Company is not in compliance with the minimum bid price requirement for continued listing on The Nasdaq Capital Market, as set forth in Nasdaq Marketplace Listing Rule&#160;5550(a)(2) (the &#8220;Minimum Bid Price Requirement&#8221;). </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In accordance with&#160;Nasdaq Marketplace Rule 5810(c)(3)(A), the Company has&#160;a period of 180 calendar days from January&#160;9, 2025, or&#160;until July&#160;8, 2025, to regain compliance with the&#160;Minimum Bid Price Requirement.&#160;If at any time before July&#160;8, 2025, the closing bid price of the Company&#8217;s common stock closes at or above $1.00 per share for a minimum of&#160;10 consecutive trading days,&#160;Nasdaq will provide written notification that the Company has achieved compliance with the Minimum Bid Price Requirement, and the matter will be resolved. If the Company does not regain compliance&#160;during the compliance period ending on July&#160;8, 2025, then Nasdaq may grant the Company a second 180 calendar day grace period to regain compliance, provided the Company (i)&#160;meets the continued listing requirement for market value of publicly-held shares and all other initial listing standards for The Nasdaq Capital Market, other than the Minimum Bid Price Requirement, and (ii)&#160;the Company notifies Nasdaq of its intent to cure the&#160;deficiency. </p> <p style="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">The Company intends to continue actively monitoring the closing bid price for the Company&#8217;s common stock between now and July&#160;8, 2025, and will consider available options to resolve the deficiency and regain compliance with the Minimum Bid Price Requirement. If the Company does not regain compliance within the allotted compliance period, including any extensions that may be granted by Nasdaq, Nasdaq will provide notice that the Company&#8217;s common stock will be subject to delisting. The Company would then be entitled to appeal that determination to a Nasdaq hearings panel.
